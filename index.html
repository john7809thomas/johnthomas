Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about Dubai freelancer visas

How much does a Dubai freelancer visa cost?

The total cost typically ranges between AED 7,000 to AED 15,000, including the freelance permit (AED 7,500-15,000), visa processing fees (AED 2,000-3,000), medical test & Emirates ID (AED 1,000-1,500), and health insurance (AED 1,500-5,000 annually). Costs vary by free zone.

How long does the application process take?

The entire process typically takes 2-4 weeks from initial application to visa stamping. The freelance permit approval takes 5-7 working days, while the visa processing after medical tests takes about 10-14 days.

Can I sponsor my family with a freelancer visa?

Yes, once you have your residency visa, you can sponsor your spouse, children, and in some cases, even your parents. You'll need to meet minimum salary requirements (typically AED 4,000-5,000 per month) and provide accommodation proof.

Do I need a degree to get a freelancer visa?

Requirements vary by free zone. Some zones (like DIFC) require a bachelor's degree, while others accept substantial professional experience in lieu of formal education. Media and creative fields often prioritize portfolio over degrees.
